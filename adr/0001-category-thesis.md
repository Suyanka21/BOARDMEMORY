# ADR 0001 — Category Thesis: Enterprise Decision Memory

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2026-07-11
**Deciders:** Founder
**Traces To:** Strategic Foundation Document §3, §10; DECISION_FREEZE.md §Category; PRODUCT_CONSTITUTION.md §Mission

---

## Context

When positioning a new product in the enterprise software landscape, the founding team evaluated multiple existing categories:

- **RegTech** — focused on regulatory compliance automation
- **LegalTech** — focused on legal workflow digitization
- **Governance Software** — focused on board portal functionality
- **Knowledge Management** — focused on enterprise wikis and knowledge bases
- **Document Management** — focused on file storage and retrieval

Research across governance, procurement, trade, licensing, taxation, and compliance revealed a recurring, unaddressed failure: **organizations preserve documents but lose the reasoning behind decisions.** This problem — institutional memory loss — is not adequately addressed by any existing category.

Existing categories preserve *artifacts* (documents, contracts, compliance records). None preserve *reasoning* (why a decision was made, what assumptions supported it, what alternatives were considered, what risks were accepted).

## Decision

**Define and build a new category: Enterprise Decision Memory.**

Enterprise Decision Memory is software that preserves organizational reasoning, assumptions, approvals, and institutional knowledge so that decisions become reusable rather than repeatedly recreated.

BoardMemory is the first product in this category, with board governance as the beachhead workflow.

The category is deliberately distinct from:
- Document Management (preserves files, not reasoning)
- Governance Portals (digitize process, not knowledge)
- RegTech/LegalTech (automate compliance, not preserve judgment)
- Knowledge Management (store information, not decision context)
- Enterprise Search (find documents, not explain decisions)

## Consequences

### Positive

- **Category ownership** — as the defining product, BoardMemory sets the rules rather than competing in an established space
- **Clear differentiation** — the product is compared against nothing, not against incumbents
- **Moat through the Decision Graph** — the competitive advantage is structural (linked reasoning), not feature-based
- **Expansion path** — the category naturally extends beyond board governance into any decision-intensive workflow

### Negative

- **Category education risk** — buyers are not searching for "Enterprise Decision Memory" by name; go-to-market must sell against the pain, not the category label
- **No established buyer budget** — the product does not fit neatly into existing procurement categories
- **Validation dependency** — the category thesis must be validated through the beachhead before broader claims can be made

### Neutral

- The expansion boundary (Governance → Beneficial Ownership → Compliance → Licensing → Contracts → Trade → Tax) is frozen and may only be changed with founder approval
